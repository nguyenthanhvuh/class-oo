Spring 2021
Usability Testing

The most practical skill Krug offers is a step-by-step guide to cheap, fast usability testing. Note that the text contains the guide, but the artifacts (videos, draft documents, etc.) supporting the guide are on a website of another of his texts:
www.sensible.com

Assignment 9 has 2 deliverables:

  1. A report on a usability test carried out by your group on a reasonable web site. Please choose a site:
    • That you have not analyzed previously.
    • That you do not regularly use.
    Make sure you fill in Krug's test script with the specific tasks relevant to your test.
  2. A report on the debriefing. Note that Krug has strong constraints on what this debriefing should produce. Please adhere to those constraints.
Notes:
  • Unlike other solutions, prepare your report as a self-contained document instead of as an in-line Piazza post. Post your document (or a link to it) on Piazza and be prepared to present your results with the class. Make your test script an appendix in your document.
  • Krug specifically discusses remote usability testing, which, obviously, is how your group will need to proceed.
  • Again, to keep the websites from the different groups disjoint, please post to Piazza twice: The first time, just post the URL you have chosen for the usability test. (First come, first serve!) The second time, follow-up to your first post to add your document.
